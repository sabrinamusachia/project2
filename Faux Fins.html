Faux Fins


Each year, about 70 million sharks are killed for their fins. The fins can sell for as much as $300 a pound on the black market.

A San Francisco-based sustainable seafood company called New Wave Foods, is developing a bioengineered product that could replace shark fins as a food product. A combination of algae-derived ingredients and recombinant proteins has been derived to meet the demand for a highly valued product without exploiting the top predators from the ecosystem.

“Each year, about 70 million sharks are killed for their fins.”

“Through technology, we are creating seafood that doesn’t have to be harvested from this highly vulnerable ecosystem and that is created entirely in our food laboratories. We get inspired by mother nature and recreate what people have been eating for centuries, in a better and more sustainable way,” says New Wave spokesperson Florian Radke.

In addition to faux shark fins, New Wave Foods has also been developing a sustainable shrimp product using similar algae-derived ingredients.
